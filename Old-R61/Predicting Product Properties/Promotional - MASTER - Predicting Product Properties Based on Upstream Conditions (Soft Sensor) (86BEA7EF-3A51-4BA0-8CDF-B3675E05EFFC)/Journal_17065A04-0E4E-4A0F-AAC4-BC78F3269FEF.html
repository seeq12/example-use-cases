Product Viscosity Prediction


Objectives of Workbench Analysis:

The objectives of this Seeq Workbench Analysis are to

  1. Develop a predictive model for the viscosity of a continuously produced polymer based on upstream reactor conditions.
  2. Create conditions and thresholds that enable the process engineer to easily identify excursions and make proactive reactor control moves to keep product viscosity in control.


Financial Benefits:

The current product viscosity control strategy is feedback, based on lab testing done every 2 hours, resulting in a 2-3 hour delay in making reactor control moves once an off-spec result is received. This results in profit margin loss for every off-spec data point received and the duration around it. By adopting a feed-forward model-based approach to controlling this product property, the process engineer can minimize margin loss associated with off-spec product and maximize unit profitability.
