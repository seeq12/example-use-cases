Predicting Product Properties Based on Upstream Conditions (Soft Sensor)

Problem: Many quality parameters cannot be measured by sensors in real-time and instead are sampled and tested by the lab intermittently. This makes controlling the quality very difficult leading to product giveaway and/or off-spec material.

Solution:

Evaluate the frequency of off-spec material.

Access the raw data by combining product quality data (LIMS) and process data (Historian). (Do it! View the Samples to see the difference in frequency.)

(Optional: Use Correlation Analysis to identify the signals that strongly correlate with the quality result.)

Prepare these signals for use in a prediction model, by accounting for process noise, residence time and abnormal operation (if relevant).Â 

Develop the model using point-and-click prediction tool.

Validate the model using a variety of different methods;

  • View the built-in statistics (R^2, ErrorSum^2 etc..)
  • Visualize on an XY Plot
  • Verify against previous incidents of off-spec material.

Deploy to give a real-time estimation of the quality parameter. Use to inform operations and improve control, resulting in reduced giveaway or off-spec material. (Do it! step forward to current time + half)

Quantify the occurrence of off-spec material, and the times that the soft sensor would have given an early warning indication. Assess the financial opportunity of improved quality control.
