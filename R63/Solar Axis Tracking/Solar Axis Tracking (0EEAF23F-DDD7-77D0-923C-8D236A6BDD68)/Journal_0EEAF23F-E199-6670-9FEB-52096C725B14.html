Solar Axis Tracking/Soiling Loss Calculation

Objective

Solar plants consisting of axis tracking panels are subject to tracker equipment failures or inaccuracies. All solar plants are subject to panel soiling losses. Developing a model of expected power output based on meteorology data allows for the estimation of losses related to tracker faults or soiling. This allows the engineers monitoring the plant to make informed decisions about when panels should be cleaned off, or if the tracker control module/motors need maintenance or optimization. 

 

Analysis Steps:

starting point

Do It! Find when the sun is shining using value search (limit greater than 75)

Create a training condition when the sun is shining and tracker deviations are low

Develop a Power Model when panels were clean and trackers were close to setpoint

Determine the difference between model and actual power output

Totalize lost power due to Soiling/axis tracking issues

Build a table of lost revenue due to soiling and axis tracking issues to determine when maintenance should be performed
