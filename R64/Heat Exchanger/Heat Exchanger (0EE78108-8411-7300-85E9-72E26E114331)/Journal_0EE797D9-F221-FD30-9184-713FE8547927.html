Heat Exchanger Prediction

Prediction Model with Required Maintenance Date

Problem Statement:

Heat exchanger fouling could lead to economic losses or integrity issues in the facility due to inadequate heating or cooling. Monitoring all heat exchangers across the site is time-consuming.  With Seeq, you can monitor heat exchanger performance in real-time, and optimize the best maintenance schedule

Solution: 

For this analysis, we will apply first principles equations to quickly and easily calculate Heat Transfer Coefficient (U) from temperatures and flow rates with Seeq’s Formula tool and develop a model of U-value data as a function of time with the help of Seeq’s Prediction tool.

Pull all the raw data required. In this use case, we have arranged all related signals in an asset group (Heat Exchanger). This way you can easily and quickly access your most frequently monitored signals.

Identify the downtime period when the cold liquid flow is less than 400 000 lb/h.

Calculate the U value and cleanse the final value by removing samples during the downtime period.

  • Calculate Log Mean Temperature Difference (LMTD) from Hot Side and Cold Side Approach Temperatures
  • Calculate Average Duty from Hot Side and Cold Side Duties 
  • Input Surface Area (constant)
  • Calculate U-Value heat transfer coefficient
  • Cleanse U-Value, removing downtime and outliers

Generate regression models assuming the Cold Liquid Outlet Temperature is kept at 295F and manipulate the Cold Liquid Flow (High Flow =  735000 lb/h and Low Flow = 603000 lb/h).

Create conditions to identify when the prediction value hit the minimum limit indicating the date when maintenance is needed.

  • Find when limit is achieved with high flow and low flow predictions
  • Combine to find estimated required maintenance window

You can swap this to other Heat Exchanger available in your asset group in the Data tab.
