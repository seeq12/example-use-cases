Heat Exchanger Prediction

Prediction Model with Required Maintenance Date

Problem Statement:

Heat exchanger fouling could lead to economic losses or integrity issues in the facility due to inadequate heating or cooling. Monitoring all heat exchangers across the site is time-consuming.  With Seeq, you can monitor heat exchanger performance in real-time, and optimize the best maintenance schedule

Solution: 

For this analysis, we will apply first principles equations to quickly and easily calculate Heat Transfer Coefficient (U) from temperatures and flow rates with Seeq’s Formula tool and develop a model of U-value data as a function of time with the help of Seeq’s Prediction tool.

Data gathering

Pull all the raw data required. In this use case, we have arranged all related signals in a asset group (Heat Exchanger). This way you can easily and quickly access your most frequently monitored signals.

Identify the downtime period

Identify the downtime period when the cold liquid flow is less than 400 000 lb/h.

Quantify-Calculations and Data Cleansing

Calculate the U value and cleanse the final value by removing samples during downtime period.

Generate Linear Regression Model

Generate 2 linear model assuming the Cold Liquid Outlet Temperature is kept at 295F and manipulate the Cold Liquid Flow (High Flow =  735000 lb/h and Low Flow = 603000 lb/h).

Monitor when Maintenance needed

Create conditions to identify when the prediction value hit the minimum limit indicating the date  when maintenance is needed .
