Pipeline Drag Reducing Agent (DRA) Optimization

Problem Statement:

DRA injection is pivotal to midstream pipelines to ensure on specification product while reducing operating costs with transporting additional material. While its impact is undeniable, its high cost (~$900/bbl**) forces companies to optimize its usage to focus on problematic segments of their pipelines.

Potential Savings from Use Case:

Customer X used this analysis to save approximately $1MM/yr over 3 pipelines (Savings determined by reducing DRA injection by 10 lb/hr  for a single pipeline with a specific gravity of 0.9*)

  2. Determine which segment has the lowest hydraulic room. Results are also shown as a trend

Calculate potential opportunity costs associated with optimizing DRA injection
(Imagine! integrating with other data sets like Energy Consumption and Fluid Properties)

DRA injection is a complex process that can affect multiple areas of pipeline operation. The examples below focus on simple situations with direct effects but Seeq can be used to bring in additional datasets such as hydraulic models and energy consumption to evaluate the potential optimization. 

  • Route 1: Increasing DRA Injection
    • Start Point: Segment 2 appears to be the primary bottleneck consistently
    • Determine amount of DRA injection necessary to increase Segment 2's hydraulic to be on par with other segments
    • Assuming a logarithmic relationship between DRA usage and hydraulic room, determine benefits of increasing DRA injection for this problematic segment
  • Route 2: Reducing DRA Injection
    • Starting Point: Segment 1 and 3 appear to have too much DRA being injected during a month
    • Determine amount of DRA to not inject to achieve a target hydraulic room of 80 kPa
    • Calculate cost savings of reduced DRA injection
  • Route 3: Optimizing DRA Injection
    • Starting Point: Segment 2 is the bottleneck while Segments 1 and 3 can afford to reduce their DRA injection
    • Operators can decide on an appropriate amount of DRA to send to Segment 2 instead of Segment 1 or 3 to balance DRA usage. 

Develop monitoring views to detect inefficient DRA injection at scale
