Solar Axis Tracking/Soiling Loss Calculation

 

Summary

When Monitoring a solar plant, optimization of assets is vital because any loss in the system directly contributes to lost revenue. Solar plants consisting of axis tracking panels are subject to tracker equipment failures or inaccuracies, and all plants are subject to panel soiling. Developing a model of expected power output based on meteorology data allows for the estimation of losses related to tracker faults or soiling. This allows the engineers monitoring the plant to make informed decisions about when panels should be cleaned off, or if the tracker control module/motors need maintenance or optimization.

Analysis Steps:

starting point

Step 1: Identify Training Periods (workstep) 

  • Use Value Search to determine when the sun is shining 
  • Looking through the data, determine a solar block that has minimal tracker deviations (Tracker angles are close to set-point angle) and a period of time when the solar panels are not soiled. In this case Block 1 will be used. Bring the Tracker data, Power output data for Block 1, and meteorology data onto the screen. (workstep)
  •  Identify periods when each tracker was deviating by more than 1 degree from the set-point. (workstep) 
  • Create a condition of periods for training when the sun is shining and the trackers are not deviating. (workstep)

Step 2: Develop a Power Model (workstep)

  •  Develop a model based on a time when the panels were clean and the trackers were close to set-point. (workstep)

Step 3: Calculate Monetary Losses (workstep)

  •  Determine the difference between the modeled theoretical power and actual power output (workstep)
  • Totalize Daily Lost Power and lost power due to Tracking/Soiling (workstep)
  • Using contract or market price, determine the revenue lost due to soiling/axis tracking faults (workstep)
