Pump Daily Health Score Calculation:

Since indicators are not linearly correlated, the sum square of the indicators is calculated to represent daily health score. The

scale of the score is between 0 to 11 since each indicator can have the range between 0 to 1.

Calculate the following:

Indicator List (WorkStep):

1- % time in operating beyond 30% of design curve per day 

2- % time in high temperature rise operating mode per day (step)

3- % time in low flow cavitation operating mode per day

4- % time in Low bearing seal life (low flow) operating mode per day

5- % time in Reduced impeller life operating mode per day

6- % time in Suction Re-circulation operating mode per day

7- % time in Discharge recirculation operating mode per day

8- % time in Low bearing/seal life (high flow) operating mode per day

9- % time in High flow cavitation operating mode per day

10- % time in Cavitation due to low NPSH operating mode per day

11- % time in high suction temperature mode

12 - % time in operating low drum level mode

13- %time operating outside of expected range of pump efficiency

14- %time operating outside of expected range of specific energy

15- %time in high vibration mode

16- % time in poor thrust mode

Historical Benchmarking:

The expected range of health score can be determined by historical bench-marking during when there is no failure and pump efficiency is high, i.e. early life of the pump(asset). The range then is used to create Boundary for daily score. Deviation search is used to detect deviation from the expected range.
