Transition Analysis

Calculate near real-time production-scale column HETP and Asymmetry during column transition periods to monitor for column integrity and consistent operations using the moment analysis technique.

Challenge

TA is essential to assessing column efficiency and integrity. Typical methods for performing the analysis require complex data post-processing well after the process has completed. 

Solution

Leverage Seeq to connect, clean, and contextualize data. Perform calculations in real-time. Eliminate the often delayed, tedious, and error-prone manual process. Identify drift and early signs of column degradation. 

Contextualize

Begin with the conductivity and flow rate signals. Clean and smooth signal as needed.

  • Cleaned** 

Identify transition periods with conductivity, flow, or DCS phase type signal(s). Combine the periods where the derivative is positive and flow is low to identify the transitions.

Asymmetry 

Quantify the column integrity and pack quality by calculating peak asymmetry by finding the volume changes over the left and right sides of the dC/dV curve at 10% of max.
