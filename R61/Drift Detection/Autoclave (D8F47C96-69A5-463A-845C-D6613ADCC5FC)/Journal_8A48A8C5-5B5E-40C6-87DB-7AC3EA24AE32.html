Drift Detection and Modeling

The Challenge

Modeling and predicting condition based maintenance and or prediction failures. Current PMM cycles are expensive and and still lead to unexpected failures.

Autoclaves are integral in the sterilization process for aseptic processing parts, drug substances, and other sterile materials. Vacuum test failures can reduce throughput and can lead to costly deviations.

The Solution

Seeq can be leveraged to model the PHT operation, which can be monitored real time and projected into the future to identify potential failures. 

Bring in the pressure, temperature, and operation data. We will use this to identify point of interest, build golden reference profiles, and model the drift over time.

  • Starting Point --> Filter and Chain

Identify the Vacuum Test cycles. The PHT lull period will be focused on to detect abnormal pressure changes which could indicate potential PHT failures and subsequently equipment issues.

Monitor for PHT Deviations

Detect any deviations during the PHT using the upper and lower statistical limits as the boundaries. This can be useful to detect early signs of equipment failures that would not otherwise be detected by the DP limit during the PHT.

Model Pressure Drift: Predict Failure

Create a prediction model of the pressure drift during the PHT over time. Project the model into the future to help identify an approximate time when the PHT is expected to fail the DP threshold of 2kPa. The DP threshold can vary depending on the equipment manufacture and the validation.
