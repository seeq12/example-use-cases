Transformer Replacement Strategy using True Age Calculation

Objective

Knowing when to replace a substation power transformer is a difficult task.  When do you know you have utilized the full life of the transformer?  Most transformer manufacturers define the end of life as age 40.  However, lightly loaded transformers may not reach the true age of 40 until their 60th or 70th year in service.  Without the correct tools for trend analysis, the ability to evaluate the effects of loading beyond a transformer's nameplate rating over time would be extremely difficult to calculate, making it almost impossible to know a transformer's true age and when it should be replaced.

IEEE C57.91-2011


 

The IEEE C57.91-2011 standard provides algorithms for calculating the ageing acceleration factor for a given load and temperature and the percent loss of life of transformer insulation.  In the following work steps, we combine temperature and loading data from real-time data historians with nameplate/characteristic data stored in SQL to calculate the true age of a system of transformers.
