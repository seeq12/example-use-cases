Summary

The control strategy for many product quality parameters is feedback, based on lab data from finished product sampled every few hours. This results in a large volume of product downgraded when process excursions result in swings in properties. A predicted model could be used to predict a product property hours into the future, enabling informed process tuning decisions. These decisions made to keep product within specification range can have a huge financial impact with reduction in margin loss from downgrading product.

Analysis Steps:

Step 1. Identify relevant signals from your process data historian (PI) and quality (LIMS) databases and load them into the display pane. (work step) 

Step 2. Use the move() function in Seeq Formula to delay the reactor signals by the known residence time between the reactor and the downstream sample point. (work step)

Step 3. Use the agileFilter() function in Seeq Formula to smooth the reactor signals prior to using them as inputs in the predictive model. (work step)

Step 4. Use the Prediction tool to create a predictive model for the finished product viscosity based on cleansed and delayed reactor input signals. (work step). 

Evaluate the goodness of fit of the model using Scatter Plot view. (work step)

Step 5. Use the Boundaries tool to create boundaries around the actual and modeled product viscosity based on product sales specifications. (work step)  scorecard

Step 6. Use the model in near real time to identify and notify relevant personnel when the product viscosity prediction is trending out of control so that they can make appropriate reactor adjustments to keep the finished product viscosity in control. (work step - with go to now + step ahead 1/2 interval)

Step 7. Use the Deviation Search tool + Composite Condition tool to identify historical periods of time when the product viscosity was out of control and the model also correctly predicted deviations from target. (work step)

Step 8. Use the Scorecard Metric tool to quantify the financial opportunity of poor quality production that was predictable using the model. (work step)
