The Challenge

Manufacturers are challenged with maintaining or improving profitability at a time when there is a rising cost of raw materials, increased utility costs, and a skilled labor shortage. Increasing the efficiency of existing lines and machines is a critical way to reduce losses, and do more with less.

The Solution

Monitoring and understanding the root cause of production losses, in terms of availability, quality, and performance, is a critical step in increasing efficiency. Leading companies are using Seeq to automatically measure Overall Equipment Effectiveness and visualize productivity across 100's assets. With Seeq OEE, inefficiencies are easily identified and corrective action can be taken immediately.

The Impact

A top 5 pharmaceutical company used Seeq to monitor and improve OEE resulting in $65M/year of additional production revenue.

Method: 

Reset

1. Connect to Datasources

Seeq enables you to connect to data from a variety of sources all in one place. This may include process data from historians, event information from Manufacturing Execution Systems (MES), and quality data from LIMS systems.

Identify and Contextualize

Availability

Identify periods of downtime from the raw data. Seeq has the flexibility to handle different signal input types.

  • Line 1 - Machine Running Signal
  • Line 2 - Unit Produced Count 

Contextualize the downtime by assigning a reason. Use data from multiple sources to assign reasons automatically;

  • Numerical Codes > Reason Codes (Line 1)
  • Text values > Reason Codes (Line 2)
  • Process Alarm/Conditions > Reason Code (Line 3)

Any downtime events without reasons assigned are sent to the OEE Capsule Labeller for operations personnel to quickly and easily assign reasons. Sharing Insights in near real-time.

Add-on: OEE Capsule Labeller 

Cleanse

Production downtime can either be planned (product changeovers, turnaround, etc.) or unplanned (equipment failure, supply chain issues). Planned downtime does not reduce the OEE score for availability, whereas unplanned downtime does.

For production stops that are planned, we can give further context by specifying the "lowest repeatable time" in which the planned stop should be completed. Any excess time is categorized as Unplanned Downtime (Availability Loss), reducing the OEE score. Having this visibility allows for sharing best practices and reducing inefficiencies in recurring tasks.

Finally, we now have an accurate calculation of the equipment availability and an understanding of downtime causes (table view).

Performance

Most manufacturing processes have an ideal speed at which products should be produced. Producing slower than this ideal speed results in less product and is categorized as a performance loss inefficiency. With simple point-and-click tools, you can identify, quantify and reduce performance losses that may not otherwise not observable.

Quality

Quality loss represents the products that are defective and scrapped during production. However and wherever defect products are identified and recorded in your process, with connectivity to many different data sources and flexible tools, Seeq can quantify these losses to create a standardized metric.

Note that, in some organizations, the standard OEE calculation will not be the most useful metric. The same method described can create alternative metrics such as OA (Operational Availability).
